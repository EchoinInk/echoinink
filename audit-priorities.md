# Echo in Ink — Audit Priorities

## Primary audit goal

Assess how close Echo in Ink is to a commercially polished, visually cohesive, launch-ready premium creative studio website.

Protect the dark cinematic editorial identity. Prioritise refinement, cohesion, hierarchy, clarity, conversion confidence, mobile quality, and system consistency over redesign.

## Page status

### Near final pages

- Home
- Studio
- Contact
- Lumo case study

These should be reviewed as polished, launch-oriented experiences. Identify only meaningful issues that affect clarity, premium feel, conversion, accessibility, responsive quality, or visual cohesion.

### Final-stage pages

- Identity
- Booking

These are close, but may need final hierarchy, spacing, CTA, flow, or mobile refinement.

### First-draft pages

- Sessions
- Systems
- Archive
- Works
- Worlds

These should be critiqued more openly. Assess whether they belong to the same Echo in Ink world as the near-final pages and identify the highest-leverage steps needed to complete them.

## Critical review themes

- Dark cinematic editorial cohesion
- Commercial clarity without generic agency language
- Hero composition and image crops
- Typography hierarchy and readability
- Section rhythm and negative space
- CTA visibility and conversion pathways
- Card, border, glow, and surface consistency
- Desktop-to-mobile composition
- Design-system alignment
- Difference between intentional bespoke art direction and accidental visual drift

## Do not recommend

- Generic SaaS styling
- Bright utility UI
- Conventional agency templates
- Excessive cards or dashboards
- Flattening the atmosphere into plain minimalism
- Removing editorial negative space without evidence
- Rebuilding the site unless a true structural issue requires it
